# Personas

*Lightweight personas for the primary ICP: independent service freelancers in the $50k–$150k revenue range.*

---

## Persona 1: Maya, the Overwhelmed Solo Consultant

**Role:** Independent management consultant, 6 years freelance, $110k/year revenue.
**Clients:** 4–6 retainer and project clients at a time.
**Tools today:** Google Calendar, Excel timesheet, FreshBooks for invoicing, manual Venmo/Zelle follow-up.

### Goals
- Spend fewer evenings reconstructing what she worked on.
- Send accurate invoices by the 5th of every month without panic.
- Maintain a cash cushion so late payments don’t cause personal stress.

### Frustrations
- “By the time I invoice, I’ve forgotten half the micro-tasks I did.”
- Clients pay on different schedules and she loses track of who owes what.
- Chasing payments makes her feel unprofessional, so she delays doing it.

### Behaviors
- Logs time at the end of the week from memory and calendar.
- Waits until an invoice is 7+ days overdue before following up.
- Books new work even when existing clients owe money because she avoids confrontation.

---

## Persona 2: Diego, the Part-Time Creative

**Role:** Freelance graphic designer, 3 years freelance, $55k/year revenue.
**Clients:** 2–4 clients at a time, mostly small agencies and startups.
**Tools today:** Toggl Track, Notion project notes, Wave invoices, PayPal.

### Goals
- Bill for all the revision rounds and “quick favors” clients ask for.
- Look professional to bigger clients so he can raise rates.
- Get paid within two weeks so personal cash flow is predictable.

### Frustrations
- Revision requests happen in Slack and never make it into the timesheet.
- Invoice templates feel clunky and take too long to customize.
- Clients open invoices but don’t pay; he doesn’t know if they saw the reminder.

### Behaviors
- Tracks some time live, but forgets when deep in creative work.
- Sends invoices in batches every two weeks instead of as work completes.
- Asks for 50% upfront on larger projects but doesn’t enforce it consistently.

## Persona 3: Priya, the Growing Fractional Operator

**Role:** Fractional marketing ops lead, 4 years freelance, $140k/year revenue.
**Clients:** 2–3 retained clients plus one-off projects.
**Tools today:** Harvest, HubSpot, Google Sheets P&L, Stripe invoices, Calendly.

### Goals
- Scale from solo operator to small practice without hiring a bookkeeper yet.
- Understand which clients and engagements are most profitable.
- Reduce time-to-payment because retainers cover most of her personal overhead.

### Frustrations
- She has data in three tools and still reconciles revenue by hand.
- Late retainers throw off her monthly household budget.
- She wants to offer packages and upsells but has no visibility into utilization.

### Behaviors
- Blocks admin time every Friday afternoon.
- Uses calendar integrations but still manually copies billable events into Harvest.
- Reviews outstanding invoices every Monday and ranks clients by overdue amount.
