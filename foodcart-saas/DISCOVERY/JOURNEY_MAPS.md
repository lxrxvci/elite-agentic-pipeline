# Journey Maps — Foodcart SaaS

> **⚠️ Synthesized / hypothesis:** This journey map is based on simulated interviews and the product brief. It represents a current-state hypothesis for a no-website or under-served website operator and should be validated with real customer shadowing.

## Journey: Creating and Maintaining an Online Presence

### Persona: Maria — The Time-Starved Cart Owner

| Stage | Actions | Thoughts | Feelings | Pain Points | Opportunities |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Aware / Feel the need** | Customer asks "Do you have a website?" or catering client asks for a link. | "I know I need something, but I don't have time to figure it out." | Anxious, resigned | No clear starting point; afraid of getting locked into a hard tool. | Landing page that explains "website in 10 minutes from your existing links." |
| **2. Evaluate options** | Searches "food cart website builder," asks other cart owners, looks at Wix/Squarespace/Linktree. | "These are either too expensive, too generic, or too much work." | Overwhelmed, skeptical | Pricing confusion; feature overload; generic restaurant templates. | Transparent, cart-specific pricing; template previews matched to cuisine/vibe. |
| **3. Gather content** | Hunts through phone photos, old menus, Google Business, Instagram, DoorDash. | "Why do I have to type all this again?" | Frustrated | Content scattered; no canonical menu; images not optimized. | Auto-import from Google Business Profile, Yelp, DoorDash, Instagram, menu URL. |
| **4. Build / design** | Picks a template, tries to customize colors/fonts, uploads photos. | "This doesn't look like us." | Disappointed | Limited customization; generic look; mobile editor is clunky. | Opinionated templates + easy brand customization; mobile-first editor. |
| **5. Publish / share** | Copies link to Instagram bio, prints QR code for cart, tells staff. | "I hope this is right." | Cautiously hopeful | Fear that hours/menu are wrong; no easy way to update. | One-click publish; preview URL; integrated QR code generator. |
| **6. Maintain over time** | Hours change due to weather/pod schedule; menu specials rotate; sold-out items. | "I should update the site, but Instagram is faster." | Guilty, annoyed | Updating is slow; site becomes stale; customers rely on outdated info. | AI assistant for fast edits ("Friday hours to 11pm"); auto-sync from Google; version history + revert. |
| **7. Capture orders / catering** | Customer clicks order link; catering inquiry arrives. | "Did they click DoorDash or our direct link?" | Curious, margin-focused | No visibility into channel performance; catering leads lost in DMs. | Direct order analytics; structured catering form; CRM-style inquiry list. |
| **8. Iterate / grow** | Wants to add catering page, holiday hours, custom domain. | "Can this grow with me?" | Hopeful but unsure | Custom domain and advanced features feel like an upsell trap. | Clear roadmap; custom domain as future upgrade; scalable sections. |

## Emotional Arc

```
Anxious → Overwhelmed → Frustrated → Disappointed → Cautiously Hopeful → Guilty → Curious → Hopeful
```

The biggest emotional dips occur during **evaluation**, **content gathering**, and **ongoing maintenance**. The product's core value proposition should directly address these three stages.

## Key Moments of Truth

| Moment | Why It Matters | Product Response |
|---|---|---|
| **First impression (landing page)** | Determines whether a time-poor operator even tries the product. | Clear, cart-specific value prop; social proof from other cart owners. |
| **Onboarding data import** | If import fails, the user must manually retype everything and may abandon. | Robust link ingestion with fallback to manual entry and clear progress indicator. |
| **Template selection** | Wrong template = site feels inauthentic and damages trust. | Template quiz/preview; cuisine/vibe-matched defaults; easy customization. |
| **First edit after publish** | This is where most tools lose users. | AI assistant or one-tap mobile edit with preview. |
| **Catering / direct order conversion** | This is where revenue impact is felt. | Prominent CTAs, analytics, and structured forms. |

## Journey: Catering Inquiry (Current State)

| Stage | Actions | Pain Points | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Customer sees catering mention | Texts, DMs, or emails operator | Channel fragmentation | Dedicated catering section with clear CTA |
| Operator receives inquiry | Responds while cooking; details get buried | Slow response, lost context | Structured form asks date, headcount, dietary needs, budget |
| Back-and-forth negotiation | Repeats same questions | Time waste, unprofessional feel | Auto-reply with package options / PDF menu |
| Booking confirmation | Manual calendar/notes | Double-booking risk | Integration with operator's calendar (future) |

## Future-State Vision

In the ideal future state, Maria:

1. Signs up with her Google Business Profile link.
2. Reviews a generated site in under 5 minutes on her phone.
3. Publishes and drops the link in her Instagram bio.
4. Updates Friday hours by typing "Friday hours to 11pm" and approving the diff.
5. Receives catering inquiries through a structured form instead of scattered DMs.
6. Tracks how many customers click "Order Online" vs. delivery apps.

This future state becomes the product's experience vision and shapes the shaped bets in the Opportunity Solution Tree.
