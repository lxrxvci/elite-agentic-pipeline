## Business Context

- Cart open Tue–Sun, hours shift based on pod schedule and weather.
- Menu: ~12 items, 3 weekly specials, vegetarian options.
- Sales channels: in-person, Square POS, DoorDash, Instagram DMs for catering.
- Online presence: Instagram (active, stories daily), Facebook page (neglected), Google Business Profile (created by a cousin, rarely updated).
- No website. "I tried Wix once but it sat blank for three months."

## Jobs, Pains, and Gains

### Jobs

- Keep customers informed of **current location and hours** because the cart moves.
- Make the business look **legitimate enough** to book small catering gigs (office lunches, birthdays).
- Take **direct orders** to reduce DoorDash commission.

### Pains

- Updating hours on Instagram, Google, and DoorDash separately is exhausting and error-prone.
- Customers show up at the old location or after closing and leave bad Google reviews.
- Catering requests come through scattered DMs; she loses details and forgets to follow up.
- Doesn’t know how to write "website copy"; wants the food to speak for itself.

### Gains

- A single place to send people that has the menu, hours, location, and order links.
- Something that feels warm and family-owned, not corporate.
- Less phone time answering "Are you open?" and "Do you cater?"

## Key Quotes

> "If I send someone a link, it needs to work on their phone in 2 seconds. I don't have time to teach people how to use my website."

> "I want it to look like us — bright, a little messy, like my mom’s kitchen. Not like a Chipotle app."

> "Friday hours change in winter. If the website is wrong even once, people will never trust it again."

> "Catering is where the real money is, but right now it’s all 'DM me' and then I forget."

## Sean Ellis Product/Market Fit Question

> **"How would you feel if you could no longer use a service that gave you a generated restaurant website with your menu, hours, and order links?"**

- Answer: **"Somewhat disappointed."**
- Qualitative note: Wants the outcome, but only if it is truly low-maintenance. Has been burned by tools that promise easy and then require constant upkeep.

## Design / Template Reaction

- Shown screenshots: Banh Mi Fusion (bold/diagonal/yellow), Real Indian Food (navy/saffron/gold), Mis Abuelos (family/Mexican warmth).
- Immediate preference: **Mis Abuelos** concept — "feels like a family place."
- Also liked Banh Mi Fusion’s big buttons and "OPEN NOW" badge, but worried yellow was "too loud for us."
- Strong desire for **color customization**: "Can I make it red and green?"

## Assumptions / Hypotheses to Validate

- Operators with rotating locations will treat live hours/location as the #1 must-have.
- A warm, family-style template outperforms a bold/branded template for Mexican/Latinx carts.
- Catering form is a high-value, low-effort conversion point.
