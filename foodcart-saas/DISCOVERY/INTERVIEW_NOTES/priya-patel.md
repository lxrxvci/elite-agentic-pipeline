## Business Context

- Cart open 6 days/week; catering on weekends and weekdays for corporate/office events.
- Menu: 50+ items, curries, vegan/gluten-free clearly marked; daily specials.
- Sales channels: in-person, DoorDash, catering inquiry via text/email.
- Online presence: Google Business Profile (outdated), Facebook page, no website.
- All menu photos are on her phone; no canonical PDF or hosted menu.

## Jobs, Pains, and Gains

### Jobs

- Communicate **heritage and authenticity** to compete with generic Indian fast-casual chains.
- Capture and organize **catering inquiries** without losing details in text threads.
- Keep menu information accurate (dietary tags, daily specials) for health-conscious and vegan customers.

### Pains

- Catering requests come in through random channels; she has to re-ask the same questions.
- Customers call to ask "Is it gluten-free?" because the DoorDash description is too short.
- Does not trust herself (or a machine) to update the website without double-checking every word.
- Worried that AI might change prices or delete items by accident.

### Gains

- A site that tells the family story and explains the food with care.
- A simple catering form that asks: date, headcount, dietary needs, budget.
- Confidence that any change is reviewed before it goes live.

## Key Quotes

> "Our food is not fast food. I need people to understand that when they land on the page."

> "If a computer changes my menu and I don't see it first, I will have a panic attack."

> "Catering customers want to trust you. A real website with our story makes us look serious."

> "I don't care about fancy animation. I care that my daughter can update it when I'm cooking."

## Sean Ellis Product/Market Fit Question

> **"How would you feel if you could no longer use a service that gave you a generated restaurant website with your menu, hours, and order links?"**

- Answer: **"Somewhat disappointed."**
- Qualitative note: Interested, but trust/control is a prerequisite. Would not use AI edit without preview/approval.

## Design / Template Reaction

- Shown screenshots: Banh Mi Fusion, Real Indian Food, Mis Abuelos.
- Immediate preference: **Real Indian Food** — "This feels like us. Warm, respectful, not shouting."
- Liked the story section and the framed food image.
- Wanted dietary icons (vegan, gluten-free) prominently shown.

## Assumptions / Hypotheses to Validate

- Catering workflow is a distinct, higher-value job than a la carte ordering for some carts.
- Preview + approval is a non-negotiable trust feature for risk-averse operators.
- Heritage/storytelling templates increase perceived legitimacy for family-run ethnic restaurants.

## Next Steps

- Prototype a catering form and test with Priya-like operators.
- Conduct a trust-focused usability test of the AI assistant's preview/approval flow.
