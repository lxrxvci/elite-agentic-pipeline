# Interview Notes: Diego — Barbacoa Cart Owner

**Date:** 2026-06-24  
**Interviewer:** UX Researcher (synthetic baseline for discovery)  
**Business:** Mis Abuelos Barbacoa — family-run food cart  
**Role:** Owner/operator  
**Employees:** 3 (family members)  
**Annual revenue estimate:** ~$200K  
**Current online presence:** Facebook, TikTok, no website  

## Jobs
- Honor family tradition and tell the generational story behind the food.
- Let customers know where the cart will be (rotates between two spots).
- Take pre-orders for weekend barbacoa by the pound.

## Pains
- "Our food is special, but online we look like every other cart."
- Location changes weekly; customers message constantly asking "where are you today?"
- No place to publish the weekly pre-order menu.

## Gains
- Wants a beautiful, warm site that feels like family/Mexican heritage.
- Wants to update location and hours easily from his phone.
- Pre-order form would be a game-changer for weekend sales.

## Direct Quotes
- "I want the website to feel like my abuela's kitchen, not a tech company."
- "If I can text the update, I'll use it every week."
