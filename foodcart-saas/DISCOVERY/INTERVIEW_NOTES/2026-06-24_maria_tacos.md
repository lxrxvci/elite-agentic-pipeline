# Interview Notes: Maria — Taco Cart Owner

**Date:** 2026-06-24  
**Interviewer:** UX Researcher (synthetic baseline for discovery)  
**Business:** Maria's Tacos — single food cart, Portland, OR  
**Role:** Owner/operator  
**Employees:** 1 (herself, plus occasional helper)  
**Annual revenue estimate:** ~$180K  
**Current online presence:** Instagram only; no website  

## Jobs
- Get found by office workers and event organizers looking for lunch/catering.
- Post daily specials and sold-out items quickly.
- Look legit when event organizers ask for a "menu link."

## Pains
- "I send my Instagram link to everyone, but not everyone has Instagram."
- Catering requests come through DMs and get buried; she's lost at least two leads this year.
- Tried Wix once, spent 3 hours, gave up because it "didn't look like me."
- Hours change with weather; customers show up when she's closed.

## Gains
- Wants a one-page site with menu, hours, and a "book catering" button.
- Loves the idea of typing "Add vegan al pastor" and having it update everywhere.
- Would pay "maybe $25 a month if it actually stayed updated without me doing everything."

## Direct Quotes
- "If I can't update it from my phone in 30 seconds, I won't use it."
- "I don't trust robots, but if it shows me what it's going to change first, okay."
