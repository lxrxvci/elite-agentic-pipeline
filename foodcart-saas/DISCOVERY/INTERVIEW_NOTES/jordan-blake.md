## Business Context

- Bakery open 7 days; cart only on weekends at 2 farmers markets.
- Menu changes daily based on what's fresh; sells out by noon.
- Sales channels: in-person, Square Online store, Instagram, wholesale to 3 coffee shops.
- Online presence: Squarespace site (started at $16/mo, now $29/mo), Instagram, Google Business.
- Already pays for a website but feels it's overkill and hard to edit.

## Jobs, Pains, and Gains

### Jobs

- Post **today's menu and sold-out status** quickly each morning.
- Look local/craft-focused to attract farmers-market shoppers.
- Keep costs down; current Squarespace feels expensive for what they use.

### Pains

- Squarespace editor is slow on mobile; they run the business from a phone.
- Updating sold-out items is clunky; they often just post to Instagram instead.
- Doesn't use 90% of Squarespace features (blog, commerce, appointments).
- Wants a custom domain eventually but not a $50/mo plan.

### Gains

- A simple, mobile-first editor that lets them update today's menu in < 60 seconds.
- Lower price or clear value compared to Squarespace.
- Integration with Square/Social so they don't retype everything.

## Key Quotes

> "I just need a page that says who we are, what's fresh today, and where to find us."

> "If I can't update it from my phone while the oven is running, I won't use it."

> "I pay Squarespace mostly for the domain. If you can give me a custom domain later, I'd switch."

> "I don't need AI to write poetry. I need it to swap 'scones' for 'sold out' at 9am."

## Sean Ellis Product/Market Fit Question

> **"How would you feel if you could no longer use a service that gave you a generated restaurant website with your menu, hours, and order links?"**

- Answer: **"Not disappointed — I have other options."**
- Qualitative note: Currently solving the problem with Squarespace + Instagram. Would switch only for clear cost, speed, or mobile-editing advantage.

## Design / Template Reaction

- Shown screenshots: Banh Mi Fusion, Real Indian Food, Mis Abuelos.
- Immediate preference: **Mis Abuelos** concept — "warm, neighborhood, not too loud."
- Banh Mi Fusion felt "too street-food aggressive" for a bakery.
- Wanted a daily-specials module and sold-out badge.

## Assumptions / Hypotheses to Validate

- Existing website owners are a viable segment if the product is cheaper/faster on mobile.
- Daily menu updates are a high-frequency job worth optimizing.
- Custom-domain support is a future requirement, not a launch requirement.

## Next Steps

- Benchmark onboarding and mobile-edit speed against Squarespace.
- Test a "daily special / sold out" micro-feature with bakery/cafe owners.
