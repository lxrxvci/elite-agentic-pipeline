## Business Context

- 2 locations (cart + small storefront), both with different hours.
- Menu: banh mi, bubble tea, fusion specials; updates seasonally.
- Sales channels: in-person, Postmates, DoorDash, own Square online ordering.
- Online presence: Instagram (strong), Google Business (both locations), Yelp (monitored), basic Linktree.
- Has a landing page built by a friend but hasn't updated it in 8 months.

## Jobs, Pains, and Gains

### Jobs

- Drive **direct online orders** instead of losing margin to delivery apps.
- Keep both locations' menus/hours in sync without double-entry.
- Look **distinctive and modern** to stand out in a crowded food-cart pod.

### Pains

- Delivery apps take 15–30% per order; direct orders are much better margin.
- Updating the Linktree every time the menu changes is annoying.
- Wants bold design but doesn't know CSS or design tools.
- Customers sometimes order from the wrong location's menu.

### Gains

- A site that matches the cart’s personality: bold, graphic, fast.
- One place to point influencers/press to — "looks like a real brand."
- Analytics on what people click (menu vs. order vs. catering).

## Key Quotes

> "I don't want a boring template that looks like every other Squarespace restaurant. I want diagonal stuff, big type, attitude."

> "Every order that comes through our own link instead of DoorDash is basically free money."

> "If I can text the website 'Add bulgogi banh mi special' and it just does it, I'm sold."

> "I need people to know which location they're looking at. Springwater closes later than Fairview."

## Sean Ellis Product/Market Fit Question

> **"How would you feel if you could no longer use a service that gave you a generated restaurant website with your menu, hours, and order links?"**

- Answer: **"Very disappointed."**
- Qualitative note: Already feels the pain of fragmented tools; values time savings and brand control. Would adopt quickly if onboarding is under 15 minutes.

## Design / Template Reaction

- Shown screenshots: Banh Mi Fusion, Real Indian Food, Mis Abuelos.
- Immediate preference: **Banh Mi Fusion** — "That's basically us. The diagonal thing is fire."
- Liked the live "OPEN NOW" badge and the menu carousel.
- Wants to swap fonts and accent color (coral → his brand red).

## Assumptions / Hypotheses to Validate

- Tech-comfortable operators will pay more for distinctive design and AI editing.
- Multi-location operators need location-aware menus/hours as a first-class feature.
- Direct-order conversion is a stronger motivator than "online legitimacy."

## Next Steps

- Measure whether AI menu edits reduce update time vs. manual dashboard.
