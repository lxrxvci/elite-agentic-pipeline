# Job Stories — Foodcart SaaS

> **⚠️ Synthesized / hypothesis:** These job stories are derived from simulated interviews and the product brief. They are testable hypotheses about what food-cart and small-restaurant operators are trying to achieve.

## Format

> When **[situation]**, I want to **[motivation]**, so I can **[expected outcome]**.

## Core Job Stories

### JS-01 — Onboarding from existing links

> When a customer asks me for a website or catering link, I want to turn my existing Google Business Profile, Instagram, and DoorDash links into a single polished page in minutes, so I can stop apologizing for not having one.

- **Evidence:** SIM-01, SIM-03, SIM-04
- **Outcome:** Reduced time-to-first-website; increased perceived legitimacy.
- **Related OST opportunity:** O1 — Auto-generate site from existing digital presence.

### JS-02 — Live hours and location

> When my cart rotates pods or closes early due to weather, I want my website hours and location to update instantly and accurately, so customers stop showing up when I'm closed or at the wrong spot.

- **Evidence:** SIM-01, SIM-02, SIM-04
- **Outcome:** Fewer frustrated customers and bad reviews.
- **Related OST opportunity:** O2 — Real-time hours/location sync and open/closed status.

### JS-03 — Direct orders

> When a customer decides to order from me, I want them to click my own ordering link first instead of DoorDash, so I keep more of the margin.

- **Evidence:** SIM-01, SIM-02
- **Outcome:** Higher direct-order conversion; reduced delivery-app dependency.
- **Related OST opportunity:** O3 — Prominent, configurable direct order links.

### JS-04 — Menu updates

> When I sell out of a special or add a weekend special, I want to update the menu from my phone in under a minute, so my website stays as current as my Instagram Story.

- **Evidence:** SIM-01, SIM-03, SIM-04
- **Outcome:** Site stays fresh; reduced manual coordination across platforms.
- **Related OST opportunity:** O4 — Fast, mobile-first menu editing (AI + manual).

### JS-05 — Catering lead capture

> When a potential catering client visits my site, I want them to submit a structured inquiry with date, headcount, and dietary needs, so I can respond professionally without losing details in text threads.

- **Evidence:** SIM-01, SIM-03
- **Outcome:** More catering bookings; less admin chaos.
- **Related OST opportunity:** O5 — Dedicated catering section and inquiry form.

### JS-06 — Brand authenticity

> When someone lands on my site, I want it to feel like my cart — warm, family-owned, and culturally authentic — so customers trust me before they even taste the food.

- **Evidence:** SIM-01, SIM-02, SIM-03
- **Outcome:** Higher trust and conversion; differentiation from chains.
- **Related OST opportunity:** O6 — Opinionated templates + brand customization.

### JS-07 — Safe AI edits

> When I need to change a headline or hours, I want an AI assistant to propose the edit and show me exactly what will change before it goes live, so I can save time without worrying about mistakes.

- **Evidence:** SIM-02, SIM-03, SIM-04
- **Outcome:** Faster updates; operator confidence; fewer revert incidents.
- **Related OST opportunity:** O7 — Harnessed AI Website Assistant with preview/approval.

### JS-08 — Multi-location clarity

> When I run more than one location, I want customers to see the right menu and hours for the location they care about, so they don't order from the wrong place.

- **Evidence:** SIM-02
- **Outcome:** Accurate orders; fewer refunds/complaints.
- **Related OST opportunity:** O8 — Per-location pages and menus.

### JS-09 — Revert mistakes

> When I or the AI accidentally publishes the wrong information, I want to undo the change in one click, so my customers never see a bad version for long.

- **Evidence:** SIM-03
- **Outcome:** Risk mitigation; trust in the platform.
- **Related OST opportunity:** O9 — Versioned, revertible changes.

### JS-10 — Performance visibility

> When I promote my site, I want to see how many people view the menu, click order links, or submit catering inquiries, so I can decide where to focus my energy.

- **Evidence:** SIM-02, SIM-04
- **Outcome:** Data-informed marketing and operations.
- **Related OST opportunity:** O10 — Owner analytics dashboard.

## Prioritization Notes

| Priority | Job Story | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| P0 | JS-01, JS-02, JS-06 | Core value prop: fast, accurate, authentic website generation. |
| P1 | JS-03, JS-04, JS-07 | Direct revenue and retention drivers; AI differentiation. |
| P2 | JS-05, JS-09, JS-10 | High value for specific segments; strengthen trust and monetization. |
| P3 | JS-08 | Important for multi-location segment; defer until core segment is solid. |
