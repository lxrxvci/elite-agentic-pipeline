# Personas — Foodcart SaaS

> **⚠️ Synthesized / hypothesis:** These personas are based on simulated interviews and the product brief. They are working hypotheses to be validated and refined with real operators.

## Primary Persona: "Maria — The Time-Starved Cart Owner"

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Maria Santos |
| **Business** | Tacos El Cielo — family-run Mexican food cart |
| **Age / Role** | 38 / Owner-operator |
| **Tech comfort** | Low-medium. Uses Instagram and Square POS daily but gets frustrated by multi-step tools. |
| **Primary job** | Keep customers informed of where/when to find the cart and capture catering leads. |

### Day in the Life

Maria wakes at 5am to prep masa and carnitas. Between lunch and dinner service she posts to Instagram Stories, checks DoorDash tablet, and answers DMs asking "Are you open?" She has no website because every tool she's tried felt like a second job. She manages the business entirely from her phone.

### Goals

- One trustworthy link she can drop in Instagram bio or text to customers.
- Live, accurate hours and location.
- A site that feels warm and family-owned.
- Simple catering inquiry form so she stops losing leads.

### Pains

- Customers arrive at wrong location/time and leave bad reviews.
- Updating info across Instagram, Google, and DoorDash is exhausting.
- Afraid a website will be hard to maintain.

### Behaviors

- Decisions are made in seconds between tasks.
- Updates happen on mobile, often while standing in a cart.
- Trusts recommendations from other cart owners.

### Quote

> "If I send someone a link, it needs to work on their phone in 2 seconds."

### Implications for Product

- Mobile-first onboarding and editing are non-negotiable.
- Auto-import from Google Business Profile / Instagram reduces setup friction.
- AI assistant must be simple and safe (preview + approval).
- Warm, customizable template family is critical.

## Secondary Persona: "Kevin — The Brand-Proud Multi-Location Operator"

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Kevin Nguyen |
| **Business** | Banh Mi Fusion PDX — Vietnamese-Korean fusion cart + storefront |
| **Age / Role** | 32 / Co-owner, brand manager |
| **Tech comfort** | High. Runs social ads, tracks analytics, experiments with tools. |
| **Primary job** | Drive direct orders and make the brand look distinctive online. |

### Day in the Life

Kevin splits time between the cart, the storefront, and vendor meetings. He tracks which channels drive orders and aggressively promotes direct ordering to avoid delivery-app fees. He cares about visuals and is willing to pay for a tool that makes the brand look premium without hiring a designer.

### Goals

- Bold, differentiated site that matches the brand.
- Clear per-location menus and hours.
- Direct order links prominently displayed.
- Analytics on menu clicks and order conversion.

### Pains

- Delivery apps take too much margin.
- Customers order from the wrong location.
- Updating Linktree and multiple platforms is tedious.

### Behaviors

- Tests new tools quickly; will churn if onboarding is slow.
- Wants customization (fonts, colors, layout accents).
- Likely early adopter and referrer.

### Quote

> "Every order that comes through our own link instead of DoorDash is basically free money."

### Implications for Product

- Offer distinctive, opinionated templates and flexible brand controls.
- Multi-location support and location-aware menus are table stakes.
- Provide clear analytics/click tracking.
- AI assistant can be more advanced for this segment.

## Secondary Persona: "Priya — The Heritage-Focused Caterer"

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Priya Patel |
| **Business** | Real Indian Food — Indian cart + catering |
| **Age / Role** | 47 / Owner / head chef |
| **Tech comfort** | Low. Uses phone for photos and WhatsApp; delegates digital tasks to family. |
| **Primary job** | Communicate authenticity and capture catering leads professionally. |

### Day in the Life

Priya spends most of her time cooking and managing catering logistics. Her daughter helps with social media. Catering inquiries come through text, email, and Facebook; details are often lost. She wants a website that tells the family story and makes the business look trustworthy to corporate clients.

### Goals

- A warm, respectful site that reflects the food's heritage.
- Dietary information (vegan, gluten-free) clearly shown.
- Organized catering inquiry workflow.
- Confidence that any website change is reviewed before publishing.

### Pains

- Catering details scattered across messages.
- Fear that AI or self-service tools will publish incorrect info.
- Does not want to look like a generic chain.

### Behaviors

- Delegates website tasks when possible.
- Reads every change carefully before approving.
- Values human support and clear safety guardrails.

### Quote

> "If a computer changes my menu and I don't see it first, I will have a panic attack."

### Implications for Product

- Preview/approval workflow is essential.
- Story section and dietary tags must be first-class.
- Catering form should ask structured questions.
- Offer human-assisted onboarding or chat support.

## Anti-Persona: "Jordan — The Satisfied Squarespace Owner"

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Jordan Blake |
| **Business** | Blake's Biscuits — bakery + market cart |
| **Tech comfort** | Medium. Already has a Squarespace site. |
| **Why anti-persona?** | Already solving the problem with another tool; will only switch for a clear 10x improvement in cost, speed, or mobile editing. Not the ideal early-adopter target. |

### Use for Product

- Benchmark onboarding and mobile-edit speed against Squarespace.
- Validate whether price/feature set can win later-stage adopters.
- Do not optimize early MVP for this segment at the expense of the no-website operators.
